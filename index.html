Hi, I'm Sebastian. I'm a developer turned product manager currently in my final year of Computer Science at Northwestern University. I'm a lover of neuroscience, biology, history, art, and all things tech. I've found my purpose to be to analyze how to get maximum enjoyment out of life, and share my findings with others. I love to see people laugh and smile.

At Northwestern, I've focused my major on both technically solving problems and making those solutions accessible to people through user-centered design. In my free time and remaining classes, I've taken a keen interest in finding out what drives people and why they act the way they do. I believe my interest in the brain gives me a deep understanding of how people will interact with a product and what keeps them coming back, and my engineering background gives me a window into what is possible with tech and an intuition for predicting technical challenges.

One year ago, I made the decision to break into Product Management, because I was looking to do work at the intersection of everything I have learned, as well as be in a position to raise the effectiveness and fulfillment of many people. I've interned one summer as a Product Manager, and I can honestly say it's the most fun I've ever had. I would say my largest value-adds at that internship were being entrusted with owning a dependency of a key strategic project, and promoting metric based prioritization. I've created this site to showcase the best of my work in a variety of different areas. Enjoy.
